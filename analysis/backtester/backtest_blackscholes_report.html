How This Strategy Works

This backtest evaluates buying short-dated (0DTE) BTC option structures (long straddles/strangles) and exiting based on an underlying index move trigger, rather than a fixed dollar P&L target.

1. Entry

  • At a given UTC hour, buy an option structure (ATM straddle or OTM strangle at offsets ±$500 to ±$3000) expiring the next day at 08:00 UTC.
  • Strikes snap to Deribit’s $500 grid: e.g. BTC at $70,300 → call strike $70,500, put strike $70,000.
  • Entry premium = Black-Scholes per leg × 1.04 (4% buy slippage).

2. Take-Profit Trigger

  • Monitor the BTC index. When |BTC_now − BTC_entry| ≥ trigger, the trade is closed.
  • The trigger captures the insight that we are trading the underlying excursion — a large index move makes the winning (ITM) leg gain more than what the losing (OTM) leg lost, netting a profit on the structure.
  • Triggers tested: $300 to $2,000.

3. Exit

  • If the trigger fires within max_hold hours: sell to close at BS price of each leg at the new spot, original strikes, remaining DTE, same vol × 0.96 (sell slippage).
  • If the trigger does not fire: forced sell-to-close at the max hold hour (typically the losing case — theta has eaten into premium without enough directional move).
  • Deribit fees: MIN(0.03% × BTC, 12.5% × leg_price) per leg per trade.
