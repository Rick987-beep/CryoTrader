Opening Execution (Phased RFQ)

How phased RFQ works for opening:
Config: RFQ_OPEN_TIMEOUT=300s, initial_wait=30s, floor=2.2%
Phase 1 (0→30s): Collect quotes, don't accept — let market makers compete.
Phase 2 (30s→300s): Accept first quote that meets the floor (within 2.2% of orderbook).
In practice, quotes stabilize during Phase 1, so Phase 2 accepts immediately at the 30s mark.
Total open time: ~50s (gate pass → option selection → RFQ create → 30s wait → accept → confirm).

Closing Execution (SL Trigger → RFQ Attempt → Limit Fallback)

How SL close works:
Config: RFQ_CLOSE_TIMEOUT=15s, min_improvement_pct=0.0%, fallback_mode="limit"
1. max_loss(70%, mark) triggers when unrealized PnL ≥ 70% of entry premium.
2. Attempt RFQ close (15s window, accept any quote at-or-better than orderbook ask).
3. RFQ always fails (counterparty quotes are 220-260% worse than book in fast markets).
4. Fall back to aggressive limit: place BUY at best_ask × 1.02 (2% above ask).
5. Post-hotfix: cancel existing TP order before placing limit. Pre-hotfix: idempotent collision.

Key Findings & Open Questions

✓ Opening execution is consistent.
Phased RFQ works as designed: 30s collection → immediate Phase 2 accept. ~50s total, reliable. RFQ quotes are stable across rounds. Fills at $95-100/unit, but we can't assess quality without mark/mid.
✓ Hotfix impact is clear.
Pre-hotfix (Mar 17): 67s total close, TP collision wasted 35s. Post-hotfix (Mar 18-19): ~31s total close. Faster close = smaller realized loss ($52 → $48 → $40, though market conditions differ).
⚠ RFQ close always fails — is it worth the 15s?
All 3 days, close RFQ got quotes but rejected all (220-260% worse than book). The min_improvement_pct=0.0% demands at-or-better-than-book, which never happens in a fast market. Options:
A) Remove RFQ close attempt entirely — go straight to aggressive limit (saves 15s)
B) Loosen the improvement threshold (e.g., allow 10-20% worse than book)
C) Keep as-is (15s hedges against calm-market SL triggers where RFQ might work)
⚠ No mark/mid price logging — execution quality unmeasurable.
We don't log the option's mark or mid price at:
• Option selection time (before RFQ)
• RFQ fill confirmation
• SL trigger moment
• Limit fill execution
Without these reference prices, we can't measure slippage or compare fill quality to theoretical fair value. The "vs orderbook improvement" metric compares to the orderbook bid (for sells) — but bid ≠ mark ≠ mid.
⚠ 3 consecutive SL losses — BTC trending down.
BTC: $75,170 → $74,344 → $71,079 over 3 days. EMA-20 ($70,185 on Mar 17) hasn't caught up yet. Strategy is selling puts into a declining market. The EMA filter may need tightening or a shorter lookback.
📊 What would help: mark price logging.
Adding 4 log lines to capture mark_price from the exchange at key moments would enable full execution quality analysis. The Coincall API returns mark price in the option chain and position endpoints.
